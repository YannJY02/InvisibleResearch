# Invisible Research

This glossary distinguishes exploratory research work from analyses authorized to support the paper.

## Language

**Shared Workspace**:
Project assets reused across research questions or publication compendia and organized by artifact role and lifecycle. Membership does not authorize an analysis to support a paper claim.

**Source Authority**:
The copy selected as current when duplicate or diverging sources exist. It identifies what to preserve and edit but does not grant scientific authority.

**Generated Artifact**:
A file produced from data, code, or a document build. Being generated, tracked, or placed beside a paper does not authorize it to support a paper claim.

**Intake Inbox**:
A local-only drop zone for unstandardized research communications, papers, and thoughts awaiting AI classification. AI may index and derive normalized records, but may not move, rename, or delete originals without explicit approval.

**Exploratory Analysis**:
Analysis used to investigate data, methods, or hypotheses. Its outputs do not support a paper claim unless the analysis is later designated as Paper Analysis.

**Paper Analysis Candidate**:
An analysis with reproducible, traceable evidence sufficient for review by the paper collaborators. Eligibility is a technical gate: it neither judges scientific correctness nor authorizes use in the paper.

**Candidate Version**:
An immutable review snapshot that binds an analysis scope to exact code, inputs, environment, and outputs. A Paper Analysis designation applies only to the named Candidate Version.

**Decision Reporter**:
A person authorized to record a designation decision communicated by its named scientific decision participants. Reporting a decision does not make that person an approver unless they are also named as one.

**Designation Event**:
An append-only record that changes the current Paper Analysis designation or its scope after explicit confirmation by a Decision Reporter. AI may propose an event from communication evidence but cannot confirm one itself.

**Paper Analysis**:
Analysis currently designated by its named scientific decision participants to support the paper. The designation can change or be withdrawn; reproducibility remains necessary but is not sufficient.
_Avoid_: Formal Analysis, Final Analysis
