[Audit counter-explanations and missing perspectives](https://github.com/YannJY02/InvisibleResearch/issues/95).
It audits the completed full-text Literature Evidence Notes and the two search
records under the map. It is not a literature-review synthesis, an empirical
model, or a Paper Analysis designation.

## Apparent conflicts that reduce to measurement boundaries

| Pair | Apparent conflict | Boundary resolution |
|---|---|---|
| Evans vs. Larivière et al. | Online access narrows citations vs. citations become less concentrated | Evans models references **made** in journal/subfield-year panels with mostly 20-year reference windows and online-availability exposure. Larivière et al. model citations **received** by publication cohorts with 2- and 5-year windows and a calendar trend. Narrower reference selection and broader cohort participation can coexist. |
| Dworkin et al. vs. Wu | Gendered undercitation vs. average per-article parity/productivity explanation | Dworkin estimates reference-list composition relative to a bounded eligible-paper model in five journals. Wu separates reference ratios, per-paper rates, and career totals across heterogeneous studies. A field-specific selection gap can coexist with average per-paper parity and an output-driven career-total gap. |
| Bol et al. vs. Traag et al. | Status/resource effect vs. reapplication effect | Both support a threshold-local bundled funding effect. Traag's multi-funder analysis finds reapplication more robust than later review-score improvement; it weakens a pure evaluator-status account without negating resources, retention, or pre-submission status effects. |
| Khanna vs. Ondari-Okemwa | Large hidden publishing stratum vs. low African indexed output | Khanna starts from active OJS journals and measures their index coverage. Ondari-Okemwa starts inside selective Thomson indexes and counts affiliated publications. The coverage universes differ, so production constraints and index omission can both operate. |
| Wang et al. 2017 vs. low early recognition | Novel work is valuable vs. novel work is weakly cited | Citation direction changes with the observation window; low early citation and high-variance long-run recognition are not the same outcome. |
| Wang et al. 2013 vs. social-exclusion accounts | Endogenous attention dynamics explain citations vs. gatekeeping explains inequality | Preferential attachment, fitness, and aging are reduced-form components fitted within indexed corpora. Their fit does not identify exclusive causes or rule out prestige, discrimination, index admission, or resource inequality. |

## Negative findings and exclusions retained

### Negative findings

- No completed note establishes that index absence means low quality, or that nonindexed work is necessarily high quality.
- No completed note identifies a universal causal effect of language, geography, gender, prestige, novelty, OA model, or infrastructure on all visibility outcomes.
- Funding evidence does not isolate a status halo from reapplication, resources, proposal quality, retention, or opportunity choice.
- Citation evidence does not support either universal narrowing or universal democratization; both direction and meaning depend on the unit, denominator, coverage universe, and window.
- Krawczyk and Kulczycki do not show that labels change indexing or citations; Settles et al. do not observe rejection or gatekeeper motives; Bosman et al. do not show that integration causes recognition.
- Database inclusion, OA, geographic diversity, and citation do not by themselves establish decolonization, scholarly quality, policy use, or professional use.

### Search exclusions and non-ticket gaps

- Distribution-only studies with no added mechanism, broad predictor reviews, and outside-scholarship status analogies remain excluded or deferred as recorded in the two search memos.
- Wikipedia/public-dissemination studies remain outside the map's scholarly-outcome boundary.
- Abstract-only or metadata-only candidates remain outside the note corpus.
- Race, disability, Indigenous scholarship, humanities, books, policy use, and professional uptake are real distributional gaps, but they do not each justify a ticket. They become ticket-worthy only when a named source changes a mechanism or boundary decision.
- Journal age, field, size, output, metadata readiness, audience size, and quality remain live covariates or alternatives; their existence alone is not evidence that they explain an observed gap.

## Saturation test

| Test | Result | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Outcomes remain separated | Pass | Discoverability, citation circulation, evaluation/recognition, production, and non-academic use are not treated as interchangeable. |
| Direct and competing families are both represented | Pass | Structural selection, infrastructure, identity, prestige, and label mechanisms coexist with productivity, reapplication, novelty, endogenous attention, local orientation, and aggregate dispersion alternatives. |
| Apparent contradictions are auditable | Pass | The material pairs resolve or narrow through explicit units, denominators, coverage universes, and windows rather than forced winner-takes-all conclusions. |
| Negative findings and exclusions are preserved | Pass | Unsupported causal and quality claims, abstract-only candidates, loose analogies, and distribution-only records remain visible above. |
| Every material boundary has a source-specific counter-reading | **Fail** | The legitimacy-label family lacks one completed counter-source defining evidence-based deceptive or integrity-related selection. |

**Decision:** the initial corpus has **not yet reached conceptual saturation**.
